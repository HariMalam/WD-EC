Single-mode Fiber

Single-mode fiber, also known as monomode fiber, is an optical fiber designed to carry light only directly down the fiber. It has a small core diameter of about 9 microns.

For more information about Single-mode Fiber, refer to the Wikipedia page.

Multi-mode Fiber

Multi-mode fiber, as the name suggests, allows multiple modes of light to propagate. It typically has a larger core diameter, around 50-100 microns.

For more information about Multi-mode Fiber, refer to the Wikipedia page.

Plastic Optical Fiber (POF)

Plastic Optical Fiber is a type of optical fiber made out of plastic instead of glass. It is commonly used for short-distance applications such as in cars and home networks.

For more information about Plastic Optical Fiber, refer to the Wikipedia page.
