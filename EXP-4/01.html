Rani ki Vav

Rani ki Vav is an intricately constructed stepwell situated in the town of Patan in Gujarat, India. It was built by Queen Udayamati in the memory of her husband, King Bhimdev I, in the 11th century AD. The stepwell is renowned for its stunning architecture, featuring elaborate carvings depicting various mythological, religious, and cultural themes.

The stepwell has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its outstanding cultural value. It is considered one of the finest examples of stepwell architecture in Gujarat.

Address: Rani ki Vav Road, Patan, Gujarat, India
